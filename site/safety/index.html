Required education · Read before using any health bot

These are educational tools. You never need to give them your identity.

A health-education bot helps you understand symptoms and decide whether to see a real doctor. To do that it needs the medicine — not you. It never needs your name, your number, or anything that says who you are. This page is mandatory reading before you start.

What is PHI — and what never to share

PHI = Protected / Personal Health Information: any detail that could identify you or tie a health fact to your name. An educational bot does not need a single piece of it. Do not type any of the following into any bot:

NEVER share — this is PHI

  • Your name or anyone else's namefirst, last, nicknames, family members
  • Phone or fax numbers
  • Email address
  • Home or work address, postal/ZIP code
  • Social Security / Social Insurance number (SSN/SIN)
  • Health-card, insurance, policy, or medical-record numbers
  • Exact datesdate of birth, admission/appointment dates
  • Photos of your face, ID cards, or documents
  • License plate, device IDs, account numbers
  • Anything that, combined, points to you

Safe to share — this is just the medicine

  • Your symptoms and how they feel
  • An age range"I'm in my 40s" — not your birth date
  • Sex / relevant biology
  • How long it's been going on"3 days" — not "since March 4"
  • What makes it better or worse
  • Medications by typewithout pharmacy or prescription numbers
  • Your general question or worry
THE RULE

Describe the problem, never the person. If a bot ever asks for your name, number, address, or ID — that is a red flag. Stop. A legitimate education tool never needs it.

Nodes are run by other people — use them at your own risk

Anyone can run a health-education bot using our free kit. We do not run those bots, we do not control them, and we are not responsible for the security of any third-party node. The people who run them may store what you type. Some bots may even be created dishonestly — set up to look like a health service in order to collect people's information, sometimes using fake medical offers as bait.

InstantHPI never collects patient health information. Not from our bot, not from any node — only anonymous, de-identified case abstracts are ever used for physician review, and only when a node opts in. We strongly recommend you never provide personal or health-identifying information to any bot, anywhere. Using an insecure Telegram bot is your responsibility.

New here? Practice risk-free first

The safest way to learn what these tools do is to practice on fiction. Open a free AI — the free version of ChatGPT works fine — and copy-paste an imaginary patient problem: a made-up person, made-up details, nothing real. Ask it to work through the case. Watch how it asks its questions and how the reasoning flows out.

You'll see exactly what a health-education bot does, with zero real information at stake. Get familiar on a made-up case before you ever describe your own situation — and even then, describe the medicine, never the person.

The mark of a real node — our blockchain signature

Every node that belongs to the Healthcare Blockchain carries the same opening signature: it points to this free education page on our official website as its first disclaimer, and it has you sign the same confidential, safe-use acknowledgment below before you begin.

That shared opening is how you recognize a legitimate node — and how a node proves it is part of the network. A health bot that skips the education and the acknowledgment is not one of ours. Treat any bot that jumps straight to asking about you with suspicion.

Mandatory acknowledgment

Every health-education bot built on our kit shows this before you can start, and you must acknowledge it:

☐ I understand this is an EDUCATIONAL tool, not medical care and not a doctor.
☐ I will NOT share my name, phone, email, address, SSN/SIN, health-card or any ID numbers, or exact dates.
☐ I understand nodes are run by independent people and InstantHPI is not responsible for their security.
☐ I understand InstantHPI never collects patient health information, and I will never give my identifying information to any bot.
☐ If it is an emergency, I will contact local emergency services or see a doctor in person.
