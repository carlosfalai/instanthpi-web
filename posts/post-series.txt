POST 2 (image: img-02-problem.png)

The numbers, since someone will ask. WHO says 23 doctors per 10,000
people is minimum adequate. Sub-Saharan Africa averages 2.9. South
Sudan: 0.04 — that is 250,000 patients per doctor. Somalia 0.2,
Burundi 0.3, Niger 0.4. Below 1 per 10,000 there is no reasonable
access to healthcare, period. That's who this is built for first.
If you're in a rich country and your system still refused you —
you're covered too. That's the second group.

POST 3 (image: img-03-how.png)
------------------------------
How it works. You talk to the bot, it walks you through the same
18-question structured intake (OPQRST) every doctor is trained on.
Then the case goes through API calls to MULTIPLE ai models that
debate it and only answer through consensus — disagreement gets
surfaced, not hidden. You get a clinical summary (HPI) you can hand
to any doctor on earth and be understood in 2 minutes. The verdict
always says it plainly: these are the answers of multiple AIs —
"they offered and recommended" — never pretending to be one human.
Your identity stays separate from the AI calls. De-identified
information only.
